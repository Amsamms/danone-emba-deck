1919 · Barcelona · A Pharmacy Yogurt

1919

Isaac Carasso

A Thessaloniki-born physician moves to Barcelona, inspired by Mechnikov's research on lactic-acid bacteria at the Institut Pasteur. [1][2]

"Little Daniel"

The brand name Danone is a Catalan diminutive of his son Daniel's name. Yogurt is sold through pharmacies as a medicinal product. [1][2]

From Pharmacy to Planet

A century later, Danone is in 120+ countries and the world's largest B Corp food company. [8][9]

Danone is one of the world's oldest food brands — but its origin is unusual. Carasso started by selling yogurt in pharmacies as medicine, because Western Europe didn't know what yogurt was. The science-meets-health DNA is still visible today in the Activia franchise and in the new Akkermansia microbiome platform.

Danone Group · At a Glance

FY-2024 actuals · counters animate live
Recurring operating margin 13.0% (+39 bps) · Net income €2.02B · Free cash flow €3.0B. [3][5]
Watch the counters. €27.4 billion in revenue, 18.5% global yogurt share, 90,000 employees in 120+ countries. +4.3% like-for-like growth in 2024, with margin expanding 39 basis points and 3 billion euros in free cash flow. Renew Danone is working.

Three Business Segments

Three segments: Essential Dairy & Plant-Based is the largest at €13.5 billion — that's where Activia, Danio, Danette, Alpro and Silk live. Specialized Nutrition is the highest-margin business — Aptamil and Nutricia. And Waters — Evian, Volvic, Aqua. Importantly, EDP is now Danone's slowest-growth engine at +3.8% — Waters and Specialized Nutrition are pulling the Renew story.

Danone Timeline · 1919 → Today

A century of nutrition and health.

Quick walk through Danone's history. 1919 Barcelona pharmacy yogurt. 1929: Carasso moves to Paris, the first international step. 1972: Antoine Riboud's famous purpose statement — "bringing health through food to as many people as possible." 2006: Egypt entry with the Obour plant. 2007: the Numico acquisition brings Aptamil and Nutricia into the portfolio. And 2021: Antoine de Saint-Affrique takes over as CEO and launches Renew Danone.

          STP

          "A marketer's competence is judged by how they segment." — Dr. 3adel

          The Marketing Concept · Outside-In

          Per the lecture: study the market before you produce. Marketing is not selling.

          Selling Concept

          "I have a product, let me push it." Inside-out.

          🏭 → 🛒
          ⇄

          Marketing Concept

          "What do customers need?" Outside-in. "يجب أن أدرس السوق قبل أن أصنع"

          🛒 → 🏭

          Danone's approach: study the consumer first (probiotic demand → Activia · gut-microbiome research → Akkermansia acquisition).

          Direct from the lecture. Selling pushes a product you already made. Marketing studies the market FIRST and then designs the product. Danone's whole portfolio is built outside-in — Activia exists because consumers wanted digestive health, not because Danone had spare yogurt to sell.

          Segmentation · Heterogeneous → Homogeneous

          The pyramid shows how Danone narrows the heterogeneous Egyptian market into homogeneous segments. Bottom-of-funnel — Activia and Danio loyal buyers — is roughly 12% of consumers. That's where margin sits. The four segmentation bases at the bottom are the lecture's exact framework.

          Targeting Strategy · Differentiated + Niche overlay

          UNDIFFERENTIATED

          Mass

          One offer for the whole market. Examples: water, biscuits. Not Danone's strategy.

          ⭐ DIFFERENTIATED

          Multiple segments, distinct offers

          Activia (probiotic) · Danio (high-protein) · Danonino (kids) · Danette (indulgence). Danone's primary stance.

          ⭐ CONCENTRATED · NICHE

          "شريحة صغيرة"

          Aptamil / Bebelac — premium, health-led, paediatrician-channel. Niche overlay within differentiated.

          MICROMARKETING

          Local / Individual

          Tailor to specific cities or individuals. Not currently a Danone Egypt strategy.

          5 targeting-choice factors from the lecture: company resources · product variability · life-cycle stage · market variability · competitor strategies. Danone's deep R&D + global resources justify the differentiated stance.

          Differentiated targeting overall, with a concentrated niche overlay for Aptamil and Bebelac. Niche in the doctor's vocabulary is "شريحة صغيرة" — a small segment. The lecture's five targeting-choice factors all point to differentiated for a company with Danone's resources and product variability.

          Marketing Objectives · 2026
